Kiana Seraj
Max Planck Institute of Biochemistry
Rringberg retreat, July 2024
Protein Evolution 1. What is the mechanism of protein evolution?
2. How do changes in a sequence mediate changes in its structure , and in turn function?
Protein Language Models

1.Like natural language, naturally evolved proteins are typically composed of reused modular elements exhibiting slight variations that can be rearranged and assembeled in a hierarchical fashion.
2.Central dogma of sequence → structure → function

Model Architecture 1. Initiate the design by introducing randomly generated population into a cyclic optimization process.
2. During the optimization process, the initial sequence undergoes the structure prediction module ESMFold
3. Fitness functions guide the evolution towards the desired structural property.
4. By incorporating PLDDT, the optimization can give not only the desired structure but also exhibits a high reliability.
Result and conclusion

1. On two Quadro RTX 5000 GPUs, after 60 iterations, the model reaches the mean plddt of around 80 with the variance of less than 0.5.
2.Being economically expensive, as it requires multiple fast GPUs with high memory capacities.
3. Obtaining the translational symmetry for dimers with lengths of 200.
4. No matching sequences between the generated proteins and natural ones were found in BLAST analysis.
